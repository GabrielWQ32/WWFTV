Tina Fey

Life & Career

Tina Fey (b. 1970) grew up in Upper Darby, Pennsylvania. She began her comedy career at Chicago’s famed Second City theater, honing her improv and writing skills. In 1997, Fey joined the writing staff of Saturday Night Live (SNL), becoming the show’s first female head writer in 1999. Her work on SNL, including iconic characters like Sarah Palin, established her as a sharp satirist and performer.

Beyond SNL, Fey created, wrote, and starred in the critically acclaimed NBC series 30 Rock (2006–2013), drawing on her experiences in sketch comedy. Her relatable humor, keen characterizations, and deft satire earned her multiple Emmy Awards and solidified her status as one of television’s most influential voices.

Major Works & Legacy

Fey’s major breakthrough came with 30 Rock, for which she won the Primetime Emmy for Outstanding Comedy Series and Outstanding Writing multiple times. In film, she wrote and starred in Mean Girls (2004), adapted from her own play, which became a cultural phenomenon and introduced phrases still in use today.

A best-selling author, Fey’s memoir Bossypants (2011) showcased her trademark wit and resilience in navigating Hollywood. She continues to create and produce content—such as Netflix’s Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt—championing diverse voices. Tina Fey’s groundbreaking career paved the way for women in comedy writing and performance, inspiring countless artists.
