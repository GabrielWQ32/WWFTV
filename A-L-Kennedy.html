A. L. Kennedy

Life & Career

Alison Louise Kennedy (b. 1965) is a Scottish novelist, short‑story writer, and screenwriter celebrated for her sharp wit and emotional insight. Born in Dundee, she studied at the University of St Andrews before beginning her literary career with a series of acclaimed novels and story collections. In the 2000s, Kennedy expanded into film and television, adapting her own work and collaborating on screenplays that balance dark humor with human vulnerability.

Kennedy’s distinctive voice—marked by precise prose and moral depth—has earned her numerous awards, including the Saltire Society Book of the Year. She teaches creative writing workshops worldwide and continues to push boundaries between literature and screen.

Major Works & Legacy

Among her major titles are the novels Paradise (1997) and Everything You Need (2011), both shortlisted for literary prizes, and the short‑story collection Indelible Acts (2018). On screen, she co‑wrote the TV film After the Fireworks (2003) and adapted her own novel into the screenplay for Sweetie Pies (2015), earning praise for her sharp dialogue and character focus.

A. L. Kennedy’s crossover success has inspired writers to move fluidly between page and screen, proving that literary sensibilities can enrich cinematic storytelling. Her work remains a touchstone for powerful, character‑driven narratives.
