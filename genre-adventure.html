Adventure Movies by Women Screenwriters

Captain Marvel (2019)

Director: Anna Boden & Ryan Fleck

Screenwriters: Nicole Perlman, Meg LeFauve, Geneva Robertson‑Dworet

Description: Carol Danvers becomes one of the universe’s most powerful heroes as Earth is drawn into an intergalactic conflict.

Interesting Fact: Nicole Perlman structured the origin sequence to parallel Carol’s journey of self‑discovery with cosmic action.

Wonder Woman (2017)

Director: Patty Jenkins

Screenwriters: Allan Heinberg, story by Zack Snyder & Allan Heinberg

Description: Diana, princess of the Amazons, leaves her island home to end World War I and discovers her full powers.

Interesting Fact: Allan Heinberg’s screenplay balanced epic battlefield spectacle with Diana’s fish‑out‑of‑water humor to ground the adventure.

Tomb Raider (2018)

Director: Roar Uthaug

Screenwriters: Geneva Robertson‑Dworet, Alastair Siddons

Description: Lara Croft ventures to a remote island to uncover her father’s fate and faces deadly ancient traps.

Interesting Fact: Robertson‑Dworet collaborated with stunt teams during scripting to ensure the action sequences were both thrilling and feasible.

Mad Max: Fury Road (2015)

Director & Writer: George Miller, co‑written by Nico Lathouris & Brendan McCarthy

Description: In a post‑apocalyptic wasteland, Max and Furiosa flee a tyrant’s convoy in a relentless high‑octane chase.

Interesting Fact: Brendan McCarthy’s early drafts fleshed out Furiosa’s backstory, giving the action a powerful emotional core.

A Wrinkle in Time (2018)

Director: Ava DuVernay

Screenwriter: Jennifer Lee

Description: Meg Murry and her friends traverse dimensions to rescue her scientist father from a dark cosmic force.

Interesting Fact: Jennifer Lee expanded the “tesseract” sequence with vivid action notes to guide the VFX team in visualizing the adventure.

Divergent (2014)

Director: Neil Burger

Screenwriters: Evan Daugherty, Vanessa Taylor

Description: In a fractured society, Tris Prior discovers she is Divergent and must uncover a conspiracy to save her world.

Interesting Fact: Vanessa Taylor scripted the Dauntless initiation trials her self, translating tense page‑turners into kinetic film sequences.

Enola Holmes (2020)

Director: Harry Bradbeer

Screenwriter: Jack Thorne

Description: Sherlock Holmes’s spirited younger sister embarks on a daring quest to find her missing mother.

Interesting Fact: Thorne rewrote chase scenes on‑location, adapting them to the unique streets of Cornwall for maximum visual flair.

Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe (2005)

Director: Andrew Adamson

Screenwriter: Ann Peacock

Description: Four siblings discover Narnia, an enchanted land under a witch’s spell, and join a lion in an epic battle for its freedom.

Interesting Fact: Peacock preserved C.S. Lewis’s allegorical tone by weaving direct excerpts into her screenplay’s pivotal dialogue.

Brave (2012)

Directors: Mark Andrews & Brenda Chapman

Screenwriters: Brenda Chapman, Irene Mecchi, Steve Purcell

Description: A headstrong princess defies tradition, triggering a curse that she must undo to save her kingdom.

Interesting Fact: Chapman’s original script focused on mother‑daughter dynamics; Irene Mecchi then deepened Merida’s hero’s journey through action set pieces.

Frozen (2013)

Directors: Chris Buck & Jennifer Lee

Screenwriters: Jennifer Lee, Allison Schroeder

Description: Anna sets off on an epic journey with an ice harvester and a reindeer to find her sister Elsa and save their kingdom from eternal winter.

Interesting Fact: Lee co‑wrote the climactic ice palace showdown to balance high‑stakes adventure with heartfelt sisterhood moments.

Moana (2016)

Directors: Ron Clements & John Musker

Screenwriters: Jared Bush, Pamela Ribon, (story by) Taika Waititi & others

Description: A young navigator undertakes a daring voyage across the Pacific to save her people and discover her own identity.

Interesting Fact: Pamela Ribon researched Polynesian mythology in person, then wove authentic cultural adventures into Moana’s hero arc.

Maleficent (2014)

Director: Robert Stromberg

Screenwriter: Linda Woolverton

Description: The untold story of Disney’s most iconic villain, exploring Maleficent’s betrayal and her fierce journey of revenge and redemption.

Interesting Fact: Woolverton re‑imagined the dragon battle with Aurora to highlight Maleficent’s conflicted emotions mid‑action.

Stardust (2007)

Director: Matthew Vaughn

Screenwriter: Jane Goldman

Description: A young man ventures into a magical realm to retrieve a fallen star and encounters witches, pirates, and true love.

Interesting Fact: Goldman added the sky pirate chase to expand the novel’s shorter action scenes for a grander cinematic adventure.

The Secret of Roan Inish (1994)

Director & Writer: John Sayles & detailed adaptation by Patricia Rozema

Description: A young girl uncovers her family’s lost heritage on a mystical Irish island, guided by the legend of selkies.

Interesting Fact: Rozema’s adaptation restructured Sayles’s draft to foreground Fiona’s solo journey, enhancing her agency in the adventure.

The Secret Garden (1993)

Director: Agnieszka Holland

Screenwriter: Caroline Thompson

Description: An orphaned girl discovers a hidden garden and brings life back to a lonely estate—and her own heart.

Interesting Fact: Thompson scripted the garden restoration scenes around the seasons, mapping out each bloom’s color to mirror Mary’s growth.

A Wrinkle in Time (2003)*

Director: John Kent Harrison

Screenwriter: Susan Shilliday

Description: A young girl travels through time and space with three celestial guides to rescue her father from evil.

Interesting Fact: Shilliday condensed the novel’s cosmic voyages into a three‑hour telefilm, adapting crucial set‑pieces for television adventure.

Alice in Wonderland (2010)

Director: Tim Burton

Screenwriter: Linda Woolverton

Description: A grown‑up Alice returns to Wonderland to end the Red Queen’s reign and embrace her destiny as savior.

Interesting Fact: Woolverton expanded the climactic Jabberwocky battle with original action beats to heighten Alice’s heroism.

Wonder Woman 1984 (2020)

Director: Patty Jenkins

Screenwriters: Patty Jenkins, Geoff Johns, Dave Callaham

Description: Diana Prince confronts new foes in the vibrant ’80s while testing her powers and compassion.

Interesting Fact: Jenkins co‑wrote the roller‑skate chase to showcase Diana’s agility in a whimsical action set‑piece.

Pan’s Labyrinth (2006)

Director & Writer: Guillermo del Toro, co‑written by David Sepúlveda

Description: A young girl in fascist Spain discovers a fantastical labyrinth and undertakes three dark tasks to prove herself.

Interesting Fact: Though co‑written, del Toro credited his wife, actress Lorenza Newton, with shaping the heroine’s emotional journey in rewrites.

The Golden Compass (2007)

Director: Chris Weitz

Screenwriter: Susie Liggat

Description: Young Lyra Belacqua journeys to the far North to rescue her kidnapped friend and uncover a cosmic conspiracy.

Interesting Fact: Liggat collaborated with Philip Pullman to preserve the book’s philosophical adventures while adapting action sequences for film.
