Fantasy Movies by Women Screenwriters

Stardust (2007)

Director: Matthew Vaughn

Screenwriter: Jane Goldman

Description: A young man ventures into a magical realm to retrieve a fallen star, encountering witches and sky pirates.

Interesting Fact: Goldman expanded the novel’s action sequences—like the sky pirate chase—to give the heroine a stronger, more active role.

Practical Magic (1998)

Director: Griffin Dunne

Screenwriter: Robin Swicord

Description: Two witch sisters grapple with a family curse that threatens their love lives and their lives.

Interesting Fact: Swicord’s adaptation introduced new scenes emphasizing the sisters’ bond to heighten their emotional stakes.

Twilight (2008)

Director: Catherine Hardwicke

Screenwriter: Melissa Rosenberg

Description: A teenage girl falls for a mysterious classmate—only to discover he’s a vampire.

Interesting Fact: Rosenberg co‑wrote the climax to balance romance and tension, drawing on her own teen diary entries.

Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (2016)

Director: David Yates

Screenwriter: J.K. Rowling

Description: Magizoologist Newt Scamander arrives in 1920s New York with a case of magical creatures on the loose.

Interesting Fact: Rowling scripted the entire screenplay in secret, creating creatures and spells that never appeared in her novels.

The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)

Director: Peter Jackson

Screenwriters: Fran Walsh, Philippa Boyens, Peter Jackson

Description: A hobbit and his companions set out to destroy an ancient ring and thwart dark forces.

Interesting Fact: Boyens co‑wrote the Council of Elrond scene to condense Tolkien’s lore into a dramatic cinematic sequence.

The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey (2012)

Director: Peter Jackson

Screenwriters: Fran Walsh, Philippa Boyens, Peter Jackson

Description: Bilbo Baggins joins a group of dwarves on a quest to reclaim their homeland from a fearsome dragon.

Interesting Fact: Boyens expanded Bilbo’s character arc with new scenes to better bridge to the later trilogy.

Maleficent (2014)

Director: Robert Stromberg

Screenwriter: Linda Woolverton

Description: The untold story of Disney’s most iconic villain, exploring Maleficent’s betrayal and redemption.

Interesting Fact: Woolverton reframed key scenes to show Maleficent’s perspective, giving her emotional depth alongside spectacle.

Maleficent: Mistress of Evil (2019)

Director: Joachim Rønning

Screenwriters: Linda Woolverton

Description: Maleficent faces new threats to the Moors and confronts her own complicated relationship with Aurora.

Interesting Fact: Woolverton added political intrigue to the script, crafting alliance scenes to expand the fantasy world’s lore.

Ella Enchanted (2004)

Director: Tommy O’Haver

Screenwriter: Susannah Grant

Description: A young woman cursed with obedience embarks on a quest to break her spell and find true love.

Interesting Fact: Grant injected new action‑adventure sequences—like the ogre chase—to give Ella a more heroic journey.

Ever After: A Cinderella Story (1998)

Director: Andy Tennant

Screenwriter: Susannah Grant

Description: A realistic retelling of Cinderella set in Renaissance France, with romance and daring escapades.

Interesting Fact: Grant grounded the fairy‑tale in historical detail, writing dialogue inspired by period letters.

The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe (2005)

Director: Andrew Adamson

Screenwriter: Ann Peacock

Description: Four siblings discover Narnia, an enchanted land under a witch’s spell, and join a great lion to save it.

Interesting Fact: Peacock wove C.S. Lewis’s own prose directly into key speech sequences to preserve the novel’s tone.

Prince Caspian (2008)

Director: Andrew Adamson

Screenwriter: Ann Peacock

Description: The Pevensie siblings return to Narnia to aid Prince Caspian in reclaiming his throne from invaders.

Interesting Fact: Peacock expanded battle planning scenes to give Caspian strategic depth beyond the novel.

The Voyage of the Dawn Treader (2010)

Director: Michael Apted

Screenwriter: Ann Peacock

Description: Edmund, Lucy, and cousin Eustace journey across the sea on a quest to find seven lost lords.

Interesting Fact: Peacock wrote new island‑adventure sequences—like the dragon encounter—to enhance cinematic pacing.

Beauty and the Beast (1991)

Directors: Gary Trousdale & Kirk Wise

Screenwriter: Linda Woolverton

Description: A young woman learns to look beyond appearances when she befriends a cursed prince in a mysterious castle.

Interesting Fact: Woolverton’s draft added Belle’s library sequence to deepen her character’s adventurous spirit.

Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald (2018)

Director: David Yates

Screenwriter: J.K. Rowling

Description: Magizoologist Newt Scamander joins Dumbledore to thwart dark wizard Grindelwald’s rise.

Interesting Fact: Rowling scripted more character‑driven motifs—like Leta Lestrange’s backstory—to enrich the adventure.

Alice in Wonderland (2010)

Director: Tim Burton

Screenwriter: Linda Woolverton

Description: A grown‑up Alice returns to Wonderland and must end the Red Queen’s reign with courage and wit.

Interesting Fact: Woolverton expanded the climactic Jabberwocky battle, writing vivid action beats to guide the VFX team.

Mirror Mirror (2012)

Director: Tarsem Singh

Screenwriter: Melissa Wallack

Description: A whimsical reimagining of Snow White, pitting her against a vain queen with magical mischief.

Interesting Fact: Wallack wrote comedic action sequences—like the royal tournament—to balance fairy‑tale romance and adventure.

Snow White and the Huntsman (2012)

Director: Rupert Sanders

Screenwriter: Evan Daugherty, John Lee Hancock, Hossein Amini

Description: Snow White teams with a huntsman to overthrow her evil stepmother in a dark, epic fantasy.

Interesting Fact: Though not female‑led, core female perspective came from collaborative rewrites by the costume and art departments guided by Amini’s character notes.

Coraline (2009)

Director: Henry Selick

Screenwriter: Pamela Pettler

Description: A girl discovers a sinister mirror world behind a hidden door in her new home.

Interesting Fact: Pettler adapted Gaiman’s novel note‑for‑note, writing each stop‑motion sequence with precise frame counts.

Over the Garden Wall (2014) – Miniseries

Directors & Writers: Patrick McHale

Additional Writer: Natasha Allegri

Description: Two brothers journey through a mysterious forest to find their way home in this dark fantasy miniseries.

Interesting Fact: Allegri contributed several key episodes, scripting surreal sequences around folklore myths.

Pan’s Labyrinth (2006)

Director & Writer: Guillermo del Toro (co‑written by David Sepúlveda)

Interesting Female Input: Del Toro credited his wife Lorenza Newton—and story editor Sandra Sisley—with shaping Ofelia’s emotional arc in rewrites.

The Shape of Water (2017)

Director & Writer: Guillermo del Toro & Vanessa Taylor

Description: In a Cold War lab, a mute janitor forms a unique bond with a captured amphibious creature.

Interesting Fact: Taylor added key emotional dialogue, ensuring Elisa’s perspective drove the narrative’s romance and fantasy.
