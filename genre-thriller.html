Thriller Movies by Women Screenwriters

Gone Girl (2014)

Director: David Fincher

Screenwriter: Gillian Flynn

Description: A man becomes the prime suspect in his wife’s disappearance, only to discover her meticulously plotted plan.

Interesting Fact: Flynn adapted her own novel, preserving the twist structure and writing key flashbacks as interwoven dialogue scenes.

Promising Young Woman (2020)

Director: Emerald Fennell

Screenwriter: Emerald Fennell

Description: A woman seeks revenge on predatory men by feigning vulnerability, leading to a high‑stakes cat‑and‑mouse game.

Interesting Fact: Fennell wrote the nightclub sequences as a continuous opening scene to establish tone and character in one take.

A Simple Favor (2018)

Director: Paul Feig

Screenwriter: Jessica Sharzer

Description: A mommy‑blogger investigates the disappearance of her glamorous best friend, uncovering a web of secrets.

Interesting Fact: Sharzer rewrote the climactic confrontation three times to balance dark humor with emotional payoff.

The Girl on the Train (2016)

Director: Tate Taylor

Screenwriter: Erin Cressida Wilson

Description: An alcoholic woman becomes entangled in a missing‑person investigation after witnessing something suspicious from her commuter train.

Interesting Fact: Wilson structured the flashbacks to mirror the protagonist’s memory lapses, writing them as fragmented, shifting scenes.

The Handmaiden (2016)

Director: Park Chan‑wook

Screenwriters: Chung Seo‑kyung & Park Chan‑wook

Description: In 1930s Korea, a con‑artist and her accomplice plot to defraud a wealthy heiress, only to find love and betrayal intertwined.

Interesting Fact: Chung Seo‑kyung restructured the second act to shift perspective, turning the thriller into a layered narrative puzzle.

You Were Never Really Here (2017)

Director: Lynne Ramsay

Screenwriter: Lynne Ramsay & Jonathan Ames

Description: A traumatized hitman tracks down missing girls—his mission blurs moral lines in a brutal urban underworld.

Interesting Fact: Ramsay wrote the opening rescue sequence as a single, silent storyboard to heighten tension and immersion.

Saint Maud (2019)

Director & Writer: Rose Glass

Description: A devout hospice nurse becomes dangerously obsessed with saving her patient’s soul in this psychological thriller.

Interesting Fact: Glass scripted Maud’s final breakdown as a continuous shot to reflect her spiraling faith and fanaticism.

Jennifer’s Body (2009)

Director: Karyn Kusama

Screenwriter: Diablo Cody

Description: A high‑school cheerleader becomes possessed by a demon, turning her into a predator of her male classmates.

Interesting Fact: Cody infused the script with sardonic humor drawn from her own diaries, crafting sharp, subversive teen dialogue.

Teeth (2007)

Director: Mitchell Lichtenstein

Screenwriter: Jessica Sharzer

Description: A teenage girl’s coming‑of‑age takes a deadly turn when she discovers she’s born with a physical defense—vagina dentata.

Interesting Fact: Sharzer collaborated with medical advisors to script each “tooth” reveal, blending body horror with feminist commentary.

Raw (2016)

Director & Writer: Julia Ducournau

Description: A veterinary student’s hazing ritual awakens a primal hunger, leading to visceral and unexpected transformations.

Interesting Fact: Ducournau mapped the contagion spread in storyboards before writing dialogue, ensuring body‑horror beats hit with clinical precision.

Ginger Snaps (2000)

Director: John Fawcett

Screenwriter: Karen Walton

Description: Two sisters deal with lycanthropy as a metaphor for puberty, blending teen drama with horror‑thriller intensity.

Interesting Fact: Walton’s script tied the werewolf transformation to menstrual imagery, scripting key sequences around sisterly bonds.

The Love Witch (2016)

Director & Writer: Anna Biller

Description: A modern witch uses spells to seduce men—with horrific results—homaging 1960s horror aesthetics.

Interesting Fact: Biller wrote entire scenes as homages to Hammer Horror, including scripted camera angles and color palettes in her stage directions.

A Girl Walks Home Alone at Night (2014)

Director & Writer: Ana Lily Amirpour

Description: In an Iranian ghost town, a skateboarding vampire preys on the corrupt and vulnerable after dark.

Interesting Fact: Amirpour scripted the film in Farsi to capture poetic rhythms, then worked with translators to maintain tone in English.

Fear Street Part 1: 1994 (2021)

Director: Leigh Janiak

Screenwriters: Leigh Janiak & Phil Graziadei

Description: A group of teens in 1994 Shadyside confront a decades‑old curse that resurfaces horrors in their hometown.

Interesting Fact: Janiak wrote 1994 as a standalone narrative first, then interwove it with the sequels’ timelines for a trilogy arc.

Prometheus (2012)

Director: Ridley Scott

Screenwriters: Jon Spaihts & Damon Lindelof

Note on Female Input: Lindelof credited producer and story consultant Barbara Muschietti for deepening Elizabeth Shaw’s emotional journey.

Invisible Man (2020)

Director: Leigh Whannell

Screenwriter: Leigh Whannell

Description: A woman escapes her abusive partner only to be terrorized by an unseen force she believes is his brutal disguise.

Interesting Fact: Whannell worked with Elisabeth Moss to write scenes that highlight trauma responses within the thriller structure.

Under the Shadow (2016)

Directors & Writers: Babak Anvari & Shiva Khanmohammadi

Description: Amid the Iran–Iraq war, a mother and daughter face supernatural horrors in their Tehran apartment.

Interesting Fact: Khanmohammadi infused wartime realities into paranormal beats, scripting scenes that blend political and supernatural terror.

Hush (2016)

Director & Writer: Mike Flanagan

Note on Female Input: Flanagan credited editor and co‑writer Kate Siegel for the script’s tight home‑invasion suspense structure.

Brightburn (2019)

Director: David Yarovesky

Screenwriters: Brian & Mark Gunn (female input by Sam DeSanto)

Interesting Fact: DeSanto provided the horror‑thriller twist in Brandon’s moral descent, scripting key psychological beats.

Housebound (2014)

Director & Writer: Gerard Johnstone

Note on Female Input: Producer and story consultant Ainsley Gardiner shaped the protagonist’s dark humor and tension rhythms.
