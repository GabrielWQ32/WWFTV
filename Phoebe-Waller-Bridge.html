Phoebe Waller‑Bridge

Life & Career

Phoebe Waller‑Bridge (b. 1985) is a British writer, actress, and producer who reshaped modern television with her sharp humor and unflinching exploration of flawed protagonists. After graduating from the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, she wrote and performed her one‑woman play Fleabag in 2013. Its raw honesty and comedic timing garnered critical praise, leading her to adapt it for television in 2016.

As the creator, head writer, and lead actor of Fleabag, Waller‑Bridge broke new ground—blending fourth‑wall address with intimate character study. Her voice brims with wit, vulnerability, and a keen eye for human contradictions. She swiftly became in demand, contributing to high‑profile projects and championing fresh storytelling in both the UK and Hollywood.

Major Works & Legacy

Fleabag (2016–2019) remains Waller‑Bridge’s signature creation—a two‑season series that earned six Emmy wins, including Outstanding Comedy Series and Outstanding Writing. She further showcased her versatility by creating and starring in Killing Eve (2018–2020), earning a Golden Globe for Best Television Series – Drama and reshaping the spy thriller genre with dark humor.

Beyond her own shows, Waller‑Bridge wrote the screenplay for James Bond’s No Time to Die (2021), adding emotional depth to the franchise’s iconic hero. She also produced comedic series like Run (2020) and continues to mentor emerging talent through her production company, DryWrite. Phoebe Waller‑Bridge’s fearless voice and boundary‑pushing narratives have inspired a new generation of writers to blend comedy with honest vulnerability.
