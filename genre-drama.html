Drama Movies by Women Screenwriters

The Piano (1993)

Director: Jane Campion

Screenwriter: Jane Campion

Description: A mute pianist and her daughter are sent to the New Zealand frontier, where she forges a passionate—and forbidden—bond with a local worker.

Interesting Fact: Campion composed the screenplay’s sparse dialogue to mirror her protagonist’s silence, using visual beats as key emotional moments.

Room (2015)

Director: Lenny Abrahamson

Screenwriter: Emma Donoghue

Description: Based on Donoghue’s novel, a young boy and his mother escape captivity and adjust to the outside world with harrowing tenderness.

Interesting Fact: Donoghue adapted her own novel for the screen, condensing five years of narrative into a tightly focused mother‑son drama.

Toni Erdmann (2016)

Director & Writer: Maren Ade

Description: A practical joker father tries to reconnect with his corporate daughter by creating an outrageous alter ego, Toni Erdmann.

Interesting Fact: Ade wrote the lengthy dinner‑scene sequence in one draft to capture the real‑time absurdity and emotional payoff.

The Kids Are All Right (2010)

Director: Lisa Cholodenko

Screenwriters: Lisa Cholodenko & Stuart Blumberg

Description: Two siblings conceived by artificial insemination seek out their biological father, prompting upheaval in their lesbian household.

Interesting Fact: Cholodenko drew on real family stories to craft the script’s honest portrayal of modern parenthood and marital strain.

The Farewell (2019)

Director & Writer: Lulu Wang

Description: A Chinese‑American woman returns to China under the guise of a wedding to quietly say goodbye to her dying grandmother.

Interesting Fact: Wang scripted key scenes in Mandarin first to preserve cultural nuance before translating them into English dialogue.

Winter’s Bone (2010)

Director: Debra Granik

Screenwriters: Debra Granik & Anne Rosellini

Description: A teenage girl in the Ozarks must track down her missing father to save her family’s home from foreclosure.

Interesting Fact: Rosellini conducted extensive regional interviews to infuse the screenplay with authentic dialogue and plight.

Mustang (2015)

Directors & Story: Deniz Gamze Ergüven

Screenwriters: Deniz Gamze Ergüven & Alice Winocour

Description: Five orphaned sisters in rural Turkey resist strict societal norms in a coming‑of‑age drama.

Interesting Fact: Winocour crafted each sister’s arc in parallel, writing interwoven scenes to heighten their individual rebellions.

Persepolis (2007)

Directors & Writers: Marjane Satrapi & Vincent Paronnaud

Description: An animated memoir of Satrapi’s youth in revolutionary Iran, blending personal and political drama.

Interesting Fact: Satrapi turned her graphic novel panels directly into screenplay storyboards, preserving her visual style.

Whale Rider (2002)

Director: Niki Caro

Screenwriter: Niki Caro

Description: A young Maori girl challenges tradition to prove she is worthy to lead her tribe.

Interesting Fact: Caro incorporated haka chants into the script’s action sequences to heighten cultural authenticity.

The Babadook (2014)

Director & Writer: Jennifer Kent

Description: A grieving mother and her son are haunted by a sinister storybook creature in a chilling psychological drama.

Interesting Fact: Kent wrote the screenplay as a one‑woman theater piece first, focusing on character psychology over scares.

Lady Macbeth (2016)

Director: William Oldroyd

Screenwriter: Alice Birch

Description: A young bride in rural England defies her tyrannical husband, leading to passion and violence.

Interesting Fact: Birch adapted the 19th‑century novella by capturing its stark prose as minimalist, intense dialogue.

Hidden Figures (2016)

Director: Theodore Melfi

Screenwriters: Allison Schroeder & Theodore Melfi

Description: Three African‑American mathematicians break barriers at NASA during the Space Race.

Interesting Fact: Schroeder consulted extensively with the real women to ensure each pivotal scene reflected their lived experiences.

Brooklyn (2015)

Director: John Crowley

Screenwriter: Nick Hornby & Colm Tóibín (adaptation)

Description: A young Irish immigrant’s romance in 1950s Brooklyn is tested by homesickness and family ties.

Interesting Fact: Tóibín collaborated on dialogue to preserve the novel’s interior monologues as external action.

Girlhood (2014)

Director & Writer: Céline Sciamma

Description: Four Black teenage girls in Paris navigate friendship, identity, and societal constraints.

Interesting Fact: Sciamma shot key sequences chronologically to capture the actresses’ evolving dynamics authentically.

Diary of a Teenage Girl (2015)

Director: Marielle Heller

Screenwriters: Marielle Heller, Phoebe Gloeckner, Delisa Meyer

Description: A teen artist embarks on a tumultuous affair in 1970s San Francisco, exploring coming‑of‑age themes.

Interesting Fact: Heller ran live drawing sessions with the lead actress to inform the script’s animated flashbacks.

The Rider (2017)

Director & Writer: Chloé Zhao

Description: A young rodeo star grapples with identity and purpose after a near‑fatal accident.

Interesting Fact: Zhao cast real ranch workers and improvised scenes based on their own stories, blending fiction and documentary.

Nomadland (2020)

Director & Writer: Chloé Zhao

Description: After the economic collapse of her town, a woman embarks on a van‑dwelling journey across the American West.

Interesting Fact: Zhao wrote major scenes around real nomads’ lives, allowing them to co‑improvise dialogue about their experiences.

Ammonite (2020)

Director: Francis Lee

Screenwriter: Francis Lee

Description: A 19th‑century fossil hunter forms an intense bond with her patient, exploring love and loss.

Interesting Fact: Lee based the screenplay on his research into paleontologist Mary Anning, scripting her fieldwork in vivid detail.

Selma (2014)

Director: Ava DuVernay

Screenwriter: Paul Webb (story by DuVernay)

Description: Dramatizes the 1965 Selma to Montgomery voting rights marches led by Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

Interesting Fact: DuVernay’s story adaptation sessions shaped key protest sequences to highlight civil‑rights strategies.

Portrait of a Lady on Fire (2019)

Director & Writer: Céline Sciamma

Description: An artist is commissioned to paint a young woman’s wedding portrait—and falls in love with her subject.

Interesting Fact: Sciamma wrote the candlelit scene first to anchor the film’s aesthetic of intimate drama and light.
