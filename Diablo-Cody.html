Diablo Cody

Life & Career

Diablo Cody (b. 1978) was born Brook Busey in Illinois but adopted her pen name as she began a career as a blogger and exotic dancer. Her candid, irreverent blog “The Pussy Ranch” caught the attention of Hollywood, leading to her debut screenplay Juno (2007). Cody’s authentic voice and sharp wit quickly established her as a fresh, unfiltered storyteller.

After winning the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay at age 29, Cody balanced writing for film, television, and publishing, penning essays and memoirs. Known for characters who defy convention and embrace flawed humanity, she has forged a path that blends edgy humor with heartfelt emotion.

Major Works & Legacy

Cody’s debut, Juno (2007), remains her most celebrated work, earning her an Oscar and launching her career. She followed with screenplays for films like Jennifer’s Body (2009), which developed cult status, and Young Adult (2011), further showcasing her ability to craft complex, memorable protagonists.

On television, Cody created the dramedy series United States of Tara (2009–2011), earning an Emmy for its portrayal of dissociative identity disorder. Her memoir Cherry Bomb (2010) offers a candid look at her early life, cementing her reputation as a versatile, boundary-pushing writer. Diablo Cody continues to inspire writers with her unapologetic voice and fearless storytelling.
