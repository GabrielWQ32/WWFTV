Comedy Movies by Women Screenwriters

Bridesmaids (2011)

Director: Paul Feig

Screenwriters: Annie Mumolo & Kristen Wiig

Description: A down‑on‑her‑luck maid of honor navigates a series of disastrous pre‑wedding events with her eccentric friends.

Interesting Fact: Mumolo and Wiig drew on their own friendship to craft the film’s most absurd set pieces, including the airport bathroom sequence.

Mean Girls (2004)

Director: Mark Waters

Screenwriter: Tina Fey

Description: A teenager raised in Africa attends public school in Illinois and discovers the cruel rules of high‑school cliques.

Interesting Fact: Fey adapted her own Saturday Night Live “Glen Coco” sketch, expanding it into a full‑length satire of teen life.

Legally Blonde (2001)

Director: Robert Luketic

Screenwriters: Karen McCullah Lutz & Kirsten Smith

Description: A bubbly sorority sister enrolls at Harvard Law to win back her ex, only to discover her own talents and self‑worth.

Interesting Fact: McCullah Lutz & Smith wrote the Elle-Woods courtroom speech in one marathon session to capture her irrepressible optimism.

The Devil Wears Prada (2006)

Director: David Frankel

Screenwriter: Aline Brosh McKenna

Description: A young assistant survives the cutthroat world of fashion under a tyrannical editor while finding her own voice.

Interesting Fact: McKenna based Miranda’s icy dialogue on real interviews with magazine editors, scripting each line for maximum wit.

10 Things I Hate About You (1999)

Director: Gil Junger

Screenwriter: Karen McCullah Lutz & Kirsten Smith

Description: A modern adaptation of Shakespeare’s “Taming of the Shrew” set in a high‑school full of cliques and romance.

Interesting Fact: The writers reimagined the heated patio scene in one continuous draft to heighten its emotional payoff.

Clueless (1995)

Director: Amy Heckerling

Screenwriter: Amy Heckerling

Description: A wealthy Beverly Hills teen plays matchmaker for her friends while navigating the perils of adolescence.

Interesting Fact: Heckerling wrote the iconic “whatever!” scene on the fly, inspired by real teenage slang she overheard at the mall.

Booksmart (2019)

Director: Olivia Wilde

Screenwriters: Emily Halpern & Sarah Haskins

Description: Two academic overachievers decide to cram four years of fun into one wild night before graduation.

Interesting Fact: Halpern & Haskins wrote each party sequence to highlight the characters’ anxieties as much as their hijinks.

Pitch Perfect (2012)

Director: Jason Moore

Screenwriter: Kay Cannon

Description: College a cappella rivals clash in a high‑stakes singing competition, blending sharp humor and musical mayhem.

Interesting Fact: Cannon wrote the final mash‑up backstage sequence after attending real rehearsals to capture authentic musical energy.

Easy A (2010)

Director: Will Gluck

Screenwriter: Bert V. Royal

Description: A high‑school student uses rumors about her romantic life to boost her social standing, with unintended chaos.

Interesting Fact: Royal based the script’s Mad Max‑style fantasy sequences on his own daydreams of high‑school drama.

Trainwreck (2015)

Director: Judd Apatow

Screenwriter: Amy Schumer & Emily V. Gordon

Description: A commitment‑phobic writer’s life is turned upside‑down when she meets a sports doctor who challenges her habits.

Interesting Fact: Schumer improvised the hotel scene dialog on set, then worked with Gordon to weave it seamlessly into the script.

The Holiday (2006)

Director: Nancy Meyers

Screenwriter: Nancy Meyers

Description: Two women swap homes in different countries for the holidays and find unexpected romance and self‑discovery.

Interesting Fact: Meyers wrote each cozy setting into the script—like her iconic village cottage—to reflect her own travel experiences.

Sisters (2015)

Director: Jason Moore

Screenwriter: Tina Gordon Chism

Description: Two adult sisters throw one last party in their childhood home before their parents sell it, with riotous results.

Interesting Fact: Chism based the final dance‑off on her own family gatherings, scripting the most absurd riffs herself.

Girls Trip (2017)

Director: Malcolm D. Lee

Screenwriter: Tracy Oliver

Description: Four lifelong friends travel to New Orleans for a wild music festival that tests their bonds and liberates them.

Interesting Fact: Oliver wrote each Fletcher Hotel escapade to highlight authentic sisterhood humor drawn from her own friendships.

The Princess Diaries (2001)

Director: Garry Marshall

Screenwriter: Gina Wendkos

Description: A shy teenager discovers she’s heir to a European throne and must master royal etiquette and self‑confidence.

Interesting Fact: Wendkos added the makeover montage after testing early drafts, scripting it to deepen Mia’s character arc.

Freaky Friday (2003)

Director: Mark Waters

Screenwriters: Heather Hach & Leslie Dixon

Description: A mother and daughter swap bodies and gain insight into each other’s lives on one chaotic day.

Interesting Fact: Hach wrote the fast‑cut montage of their swapped behaviors in a single draft to maximize comedic impact.

The Intern (2015)

Director: Nancy Meyers

Screenwriter: Nancy Meyers

Description: A 70‑year‑old widower becomes a fashion startup intern, forging unexpected friendships with younger colleagues.

Interesting Fact: Meyers wrote the montage of senior café chats based on real interviews with retirees to capture genuine warmth.

Barbie (2023)

Director: Greta Gerwig

Screenwriters: Greta Gerwig & Noah Baumbach

Description: The iconic doll embarks on a journey of self‑discovery after being expelled from Barbie Land for being imperfect.

Interesting Fact: Gerwig co‑wrote the Dreamhouse sequence to satirize pop culture tropes she loved growing up.

The Heat (2013)

Director: Paul Feig

Screenwriter: Katie Dippold

Description: An uptight FBI agent and a foul‑mouthed detective clash on a drug bust, forging an unlikely partnership.

Interesting Fact: Dippold wrote the diner scene in one sitting, scripting each comedic timing to play off Sandra Bullock’s delivery.

Pitch Perfect 2 (2015)

Director: Elizabeth Banks

Screenwriters: Kay Cannon, Mike White

Description: The Barden Bellas compete on the world stage, facing new rivals and personal challenges along the way.

Interesting Fact: Cannon added the final stadium mash‑up last‑minute, scripting extra harmonies to heighten the climactic performance.

How to Be Single (2016)

Director: Christian Ditter

Screenwriters: Dana Fox & Kristen Ruch

Description: A group of single friends in New York explore modern dating—with hilarious and heartfelt results.

Interesting Fact: Fox interviewed dozens of New Yorkers to write authentic scenes about dating app mishaps into the script.
