Lena Waithe

Life & Career

Lena Waithe (b. 1984) was born in Chicago, Illinois, and studied media arts at Columbia College. She began her career as an actress but quickly transitioned to writing, breaking out when her spec script for the Master of None episode “Thanksgiving” won an Emmy in 2017. Waithe’s work is celebrated for its authentic portrayal of LGBTQ+ characters and Black experiences.

After winning the Emmy, Waithe created her own series, The Chi (2018–present), a critically acclaimed drama exploring life on the South Side of Chicago. As a showrunner and producer, she launched her Hillman Grad Productions banner to develop stories by underrepresented writers.

Major Works & Legacy

Waithe’s Emmy-winning episode “Thanksgiving” of Master of None (2017) was groundbreaking in its tender exploration of family, identity, and queerness. The Chi has since received numerous awards for its storytelling and ensemble cast.

In film, Waithe co-wrote Queen & Slim (2019), a poignant drama on race and justice, and has spoken out as an advocate for diversity and inclusion in Hollywood. Lena Waithe’s pioneering voice continues to open doors for stories that reflect the full spectrum of human experience.
