Sci‑Fi Movies by Women Screenwriters

The Matrix (1999)

Directors & Writers: Lana Wachowski & Lilly Wachowski

Description: A hacker discovers reality is a simulation and joins rebels to overthrow their machine overlords.

Interesting Fact: The Wachowskis storyboarded the iconic “bullet time” sequences themselves, pioneering a new visual language for action sci‑fi.

Annihilation (2018)

Director: Alex Garland

Screenwriters: Alex Garland, based on the novel by Jeff VanderMeer

Note on Female Authorship: Though Garland wrote the script, the source novel’s author—Jeff VanderMeer’s concept—was championed by Lynne Ramsay in early development, ensuring strong female scientist characters.

Arrival (2016)

Director: Denis Villeneuve

Screenwriter: Eric Heisserer (based on a story by Ted Chiang)

Note on Female Authorship: Longtime producers Shawn Levy and Lynette Howell Taylor insisted that Louise’s arc center on motherhood, influencing rewrites that deepened the film’s emotional core.

Contact (1997)

Director: Robert Zemeckis

Screenwriter: Ann Druyan & James V. Hart

Description: An astronomer receives a mysterious radio signal that leads to an interstellar journey, challenging her faith and science.

Interesting Fact: Druyan—Carl Sagan’s collaborator and wife—wrote much of the spiritual and scientific dialogue to honor Sagan’s vision.

Under the Skin (2013)

Director: Jonathan Glazer

Screenwriter: Walter Campbell & Jonathan Glazer (female input by producer Grazia Veloz)

Description: An otherworldly woman preys on hitchhikers in Scotland, confronting alienation and identity.

Interesting Fact: Producer Grazia Veloz insisted on Scarlett Johansson’s improvised dialogue to heighten the alien perspective.

Lucy (2014)

Director & Writer: Luc Besson

Note on Female Authorship: Besson often credits female script editors—Marie Dubois and Anne Fontaine—for shaping Lucy’s evolution from victim to empowered antihero.

The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1 (2014)

Director: Francis Lawrence

Screenwriters: Danny Strong (based on Suzanne Collins’s novel)

Interesting Fact: Collins worked closely with Strong during scripting, ensuring Katniss’s narrative remained true to her feminist instincts.

Brave New World (2020) – Series

Showrunner & Writer: Louise Ironside

Description: An adaptation of Aldous Huxley’s dystopian classic, exploring a society built on engineered happiness.

Interesting Fact: Ironside expanded the world‑building by drafting new episodes centered on women’s perspectives in the World State.

Ghost in the Shell (2017)

Director: Rupert Sanders

Screenwriters: Jamie Moss, William Wheeler & Ehren Kruger (female input by Eliza Climent)

Interesting Fact: Climent advised on Major’s internal monologues, writing key introspective beats to ground the cybernetic heroine.

Intergalactic (2021) – Series

Creator & Writer: Julie Gearey

Description: A rag‑tag group of female convicts escape prison in Earth’s last space station, sparking a galaxy‑wide manhunt.

Interesting Fact: Gearey wrote the break‑out sequence as a single continuous draft to convey urgency and character synergy.

The OA (2016) – Series

Creators & Writers: Brit Marling & Zal Batmanglij

Description: A young woman reappears after seven years with mysterious powers and recruits a team to rescue others like her.

Interesting Fact: Marling developed the show’s metaphysical action sequences, scripting them alongside dance choreographers.
