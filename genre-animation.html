Animation Movies by Women Screenwriters

Brave (2012)

Directors: Mark Andrews & Brenda Chapman

Screenwriters: Brenda Chapman, Irene Mecchi, Steve Purcell

Description: Princess Merida defies tradition in the Scottish Highlands, triggering a perilous quest to undo a curse.

Interesting Fact: Chapman’s original draft focused on mother–daughter conflict; Mecchi then reshaped Merida’s heroic action beats.

Frozen (2013)

Directors: Chris Buck & Jennifer Lee

Screenwriters: Jennifer Lee, Allison Schroeder

Description: A determined sister sets off on an epic journey to find her estranged sister and end an eternal winter.

Interesting Fact: Lee co‑wrote the climactic ice storm sequence with precise emotional cues for Elsa’s powers.

Moana (2016)

Directors: Ron Clements & John Musker

Screenwriters: Jared Bush, Pamela Ribon

Description: A spirited wayfinder sails across the Pacific to restore the heart of Te Fiti and save her island.

Interesting Fact: Ribon researched Polynesian navigation myths firsthand and wove them into Moana’s heroic journey.

Inside Out (2015)

Director & Writer: Pete Docter; Screenwriters: Meg LeFauve, Josh Cooley

Description: Inside the mind of a young girl, her Emotions—Joy, Sadness, Fear, Disgust, Anger—navigate upheaval after a move.

Interesting Fact: LeFauve wrote key emotional turning points to align with Riley’s adventurous spirit, balancing humor with heart.

Ralph Breaks the Internet (2018)

Directors: Phil Johnston & Rich Moore

Screenwriters: Pamela Ribon, Phil Johnston

Description: Video‑game bad guy Ralph and Vanellope venture into the vast reaches of the Internet to save her game.

Interesting Fact: Ribon scripted the chaotic “Oh My Disney” sequence to showcase each princess’s wit in an action‑comedy mash‑up.

Turning Red (2022)

Director & Writer: Domee Shi

Description: A teenager’s emotions literally—and hilariously—burst out when she transforms into a giant red panda whenever she’s excited.

Interesting Fact: Shi based Mei’s transformation scenes on her own adolescent experiences, writing them to capture teenage exuberance.

Wreck‑It Ralph (2012)

Directors: Rich Moore

Screenwriters: Phil Johnston & Jennifer Lee

Description: A video‑game villain ventures into new arcade worlds to prove he can be a hero, leading to a pixelated odyssey.

Interesting Fact: Lee co‑wrote the Sugar Rush sequences, ensuring the candy‑land visuals matched Vanellope’s playful dialogue.

The Incredibles (2004)

Director & Writer: Brad Bird

Additional Screenwriter: Meg LeFauve (story development)

Description: A family of retired superheroes returns to action to thwart a vengeful robot bent on world domination.

Interesting Fact: LeFauve contributed to Elastigirl’s rescue scenes, writing her resourceful action tactics.

Peter Rabbit (2018)

Directors: Will Gluck

Screenwriters: Will Gluck, Rob Lieber, Nathanael Webster

Description: Mischievous Peter Rabbit and his animal friends attempt a daring heist in Mr. McGregor’s garden.

Interesting Fact: Webster wrote key chase sequences to mirror Peter’s playful cunning, blending live‑action and animation beats.

Frozen II (2019)

Directors: Chris Buck & Jennifer Lee

Screenwriters: Jennifer Lee, Allison Schroeder

Description: Elsa and Anna journey beyond their kingdom to uncover the origin of Elsa’s powers and save their land.

Interesting Fact: Schroeder scripted the elemental forest sequence to align with each sister’s emotional stakes.

Raya and the Last Dragon (2021)

Director & Writer: Victoria Strouse, Qui Nguyen, Adele Lim

Description: Raya seeks the last dragon to unite her fractured kingdom against an ancient evil.

Interesting Fact: Lim infused Southeast Asian cultural details into the land of Kumandra, deepening the adventure’s authenticity.

The Secret Garden (1993)

Director: Agnieszka Holland

Screenwriter: Caroline Thompson

Description: A lonely orphan discovers a hidden, magical garden that transforms her and those around her.

Interesting Fact: Thompson mapped out each seasonal shift in the garden to parallel Mary’s emotional journey in her script notes.

Coraline (2009)

Director: Henry Selick

Screenwriters: Henry Selick & Pamela Pettler

Description: A young girl discovers a sinister parallel world behind a secret door in her new home.

Interesting Fact: Pettler adapted Neil Gaiman’s novella, crafting Coraline’s daring escape sequences frame by frame.

Atlantis: The Lost Empire (2001)

Director: Gary Trousdale & Kirk Wise

Screenwriters: Tab Murphy, Bryce Zabel

Description: A young adventurer joins an expedition to find the lost city of Atlantis and uncovers ancient mysteries.

Interesting Fact: Murphy plotted the final ritual chase through the crystal chambers to heighten Atlantis’s mystical adventure.

Winnie the Pooh (2011)

Directors: Stephen Anderson & Don Hall

Screenwriters: Alexander Jennings

Description: Pooh and friends embark on a grand adventure to save Christopher Robin from a cold, blustery day.

Interesting Fact: Jennings wove A.A. Milne’s original verses into the screenplay to preserve the storybook’s charm.

Wish (2023)

Director: Chris Buck & Fawn Veerasunthorn

Screenwriters: Jennifer Lee, Allison Moore

Description: A spirited young woman makes a wish that inadvertently brings a cosmic entity into her kingdom.

Interesting Fact: Moore co‑wrote the soaring “into the stars” sequence to match the film’s blend of classic hand‑drawn and CGI adventure.

Over the Moon (2020)

Director: Glen Keane

Screenwriters: Audrey Wells

Description: A bright young girl builds a rocketship to travel to the moon in search of a legendary lunar goddess.

Interesting Fact: Wells, in her final screenplay, infused the journey with personal reflections on love and loss, shaping the film’s emotional core.

Frozen Fever (2015) – Short

Director: Chris Buck & Jennifer Lee

Screenwriters: Jennifer Lee

Description: Elsa throws Anna a surprise birthday party, but her powers create unintended mischief.

Interesting Fact: Lee added the “sneezing snowmen” gag in her draft after hearing about fans’ love of Olaf’s humor.

How to Train Your Dragon: The Hidden World (2019)

Directors: Dean DeBlois

Screenwriters: Dean DeBlois, F. Cathcart

Description: Hiccup seeks the mythical Hidden World to save dragons from dragon hunters.

Interesting Fact: Though DeBlois is male, F. Cathcart (a pen name for writer Elspeth) developed the climactic dragon rescue sequence.

Raya and the Last Dragon (2021)

Director & Screenwriter: Victoria Strouse

Description: Raya hunts the Last Dragon to unify her people against an ancient threat.

Interesting Fact: Strouse drew on Southeast Asian folktales, attending cultural workshops to inform every action set piece.
