Jane Goldman

Life & Career

Jane Goldman (b. 1970) is a British screenwriter and producer known for her inventive adaptations and original storytelling. Born and raised in London, she began her career as a journalist and television presenter before transitioning to film. Goldman’s keen literary sense and bold creativity led her to collaborate with director Matthew Vaughn on his early features.

Goldman’s talent for blending genre elements with emotional depth quickly made her a sought-after writer in Hollywood. She co-founded the production company King’s Road Entertainment, giving her a platform to develop innovative projects and mentor emerging talents.

Major Works & Legacy

Goldman broke through with the stylish adaptation Stardust (2007), earning praise for her faithful yet fresh take on Neil Gaiman’s novel. She then co-wrote the hit Kick-Ass (2010) and its sequel, showcasing her flair for dark humor and comic-book action. Her screenplay for X-Men: First Class (2011) reinvigorated the franchise with a blend of espionage and character-driven drama.

More recently, Goldman penned the live-action The Addams Family reboot (Wednesday, 2022), expanding her range into television. Known for her ability to honor source material while injecting her own voice, Jane Goldman continues to shape modern genre storytelling—and to open doors for women writers in blockbuster cinema.
