Romance Movies by Women Screenwriters

When Harry Met Sally… (1989)

Director: Rob Reiner

Screenwriter: Nora Ephron

Description: A witty exploration of friendship and love as Harry and Sally navigate New York over a decade of chance encounters.

Interesting Fact: Ephron based much of the banter on her own friendships, scripting the famous deli scene from real‑life café conversations.

Sleepless in Seattle (1993)

Director: Nora Ephron

Screenwriters: Nora Ephron & David S. Ward

Description: After his wife’s death, Sam’s radio confession sparks a cross‑country romance when a reporter decides to track him down.

Interesting Fact: Ephron insisted on shooting in real Seattle locations she knew, capturing the city’s misty atmosphere as a romantic character in itself.

You’ve Got Mail (1998)

Director: Nora Ephron

Screenwriters: Nora Ephron & Delia Ephron

Description: Two bookstore rivals unwittingly fall in love over anonymous email, blending old‑school charm with late‑’90s Internet wonder.

Interesting Fact: Sisters Nora and Delia drew on their own e‑mail flirtations to shape the characters’ online chemistry.

Sense and Sensibility (1995)

Director: Ang Lee

Screenwriter: Emma Thompson

Description: Thompson’s Oscar‑winning adaptation of Austen’s novel follows the Dashwood sisters’ journeys in Regency England.

Interesting Fact: Thompson wrote much of the script while pregnant, infusing the story with maternal insight and emotional authenticity.

Lost in Translation (2003)

Director & Writer: Sofia Coppola

Description: An aging actor and a young woman form an unlikely bond in Tokyo, exploring loneliness and fleeting connection.

Interesting Fact: Coppola scripted the film in a Tokyo hotel, transcribing her own jet‑lagged observations into the story.

Lady Bird (2017)

Director & Writer: Greta Gerwig

Description: A coming‑of‑age tale following “Lady Bird,” a high‑school senior in Sacramento searching for identity and love.

Interesting Fact: Gerwig wrote the screenplay in four weeks, drawing heavily on her teenage journals and letters.

Something’s Gotta Give (2003)

Director & Writer: Nancy Meyers

Description: A beach‑loving man in his 60s falls for his young girlfriend’s mother, leading to laughter and self‑discovery.

Interesting Fact: Meyers personally designed the iconic Hamptons beach house set to reflect her characters’ personalities.

The Holiday (2006)

Director & Writer: Nancy Meyers

Description: Two women in crisis swap homes for the holidays and each finds unexpected romance abroad.

Interesting Fact: Meyers based the house‑swap plot on her own real‑life experience, weaving in her culture‑shock moments.

Pride & Prejudice (2005)

Director: Joe Wright

Screenwriter: Deborah Moggach

Description: Moggach’s adaptation of Austen’s classic traces Elizabeth Bennet’s spirited courtship of Mr. Darcy.

Interesting Fact: She used Jane Austen’s own letters from the museum archives to enrich Elizabeth’s inner voice.

Carol (2015)

Director: Todd Haynes

Screenwriter: Phyllis Nagy

Description: A forbidden love blossoms between a photographer and a socialite in 1950s New York.

Interesting Fact: Nagy wrote the entire screenplay in six weeks, channeling Highsmith’s subtle emotional rhythms.

Portrait of a Lady on Fire (2019)

Director & Writer: Céline Sciamma

Description: A painter and her subject fall in love on a secluded island in 18th‑century Brittany.

Interesting Fact: Sciamma wrote the script chronologically to mirror the characters’ growing intimacy.

Little Women (2019)

Director: Greta Gerwig

Screenwriter: Greta Gerwig

Description: A modern‑structured adaptation of Alcott’s novel about the March sisters and their romances.

Interesting Fact: Gerwig reordered Alcott’s timeline to heighten Jo and Friedrich’s emotional arc.

The Favourite (2018)

Director: Yorgos Lanthimos

Screenwriters: Deborah Davis & Tony McNamara

Description: Court intrigues and a fierce love triangle at Queen Anne’s court in early‑18th‑century England.

Interesting Fact: Davis spent two years researching court diaries to infuse the screenplay with authentic detail.

A Room with a View (1985)

Director: James Ivory

Screenwriter: Ruth Prawer Jhabvala

Description: A young Englishwoman’s Italian holiday upends her reserved heart and sparks unexpected romance.

Interesting Fact: Jhabvala adapted her own novella, preserving her signature literary style in the dialogue.

Dirty Dancing (1987)

Director: Emile Ardolino

Screenwriter: Eleanor Bergstein

Description: A teen falls for her dance instructor at a Catskills resort, learning about love and self‑expression.

Interesting Fact: Bergstein drew on her own dance‑camp experiences to create the film’s iconic lift scene.

My Best Friend’s Wedding (1997)

Director: P.J. Hogan

Screenwriter: Gina Wendkos

Description: A woman tries to sabotage her best friend’s wedding when she realizes she’s in love with him.

Interesting Fact: Wendkos pitched the “bride‑trap” subplot from her own experiences of unrequited love.

10 Things I Hate About You (1999)

Director: Gil Junger

Screenwriters: Karen McCullah Lutz & Kirsten Smith

Description: A modern teen romance loosely based on Shakespeare’s “The Taming of the Shrew.”

Interesting Fact: Lutz & Smith wrote the prom‑poem scene as a single, uninterrupted camera take in the script.

Fifty Shades of Grey (2015)

Director: Sam Taylor-Johnson

Screenwriter: Kelly Marcel

Description: A college graduate enters a complex romance with a wealthy, mysterious businessman.

Interesting Fact: Marcel wrote the screenplay under tight secrecy, consulting the novel’s author daily to preserve tonal fidelity.

Crazy Rich Asians (2018)

Director: Jon M. Chu

Screenwriters: Adele Lim & Peter Chiarelli

Description: An American‑born Chinese professor meets her boyfriend’s ultra‑wealthy family in Singapore.

Interesting Fact: Lim pushed for authentic Cantonese dialogue and real locations, rewriting scenes to showcase local culture.

Eat Pray Love (2010)

Director: Ryan Murphy

Screenwriter: Jennifer Salt

Description: A woman embarks on a year‑long journey of self‑discovery through Italy, India, and Bali.

Interesting Fact: Salt accompanied the author on her trip to craft realistic spiritual and culinary sequences.

Bridesmaids (2011)

Director: Paul Feig

Screenwriters: Annie Mumolo & Kristen Wiig

Description: A hilarious romp through wedding chaos as a maid‑of‑honor rivalry tests friendships and self‑worth.

Interesting Fact: Mumolo & Wiig based scenes on their own bachelorette mishaps, improvising key comedic beats on set.
