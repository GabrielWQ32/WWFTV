Callie Khouri

Life & Career

Callie Khouri (b. 1957) was born in San Antonio, Texas, and began her creative journey as a photographer before pursuing screenwriting. After graduating from the renowned USC School of Cinematic Arts, Khouri wrote spec scripts that caught Hollywood’s attention. She broke through with her debut screenplay, showcasing her keen insight into character and narrative structure.

Khouri’s writing often centers on strong, complex female protagonists navigating personal and societal challenges. She has also worked as a director and producer, championing authentic storytelling and diverse perspectives in film and television.

Major Works & Legacy

Khouri won the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay for Thelma & Louise (1991), a landmark feminist road film that became a cultural touchstone. She went on to write and direct features like Something to Talk About (1995) and create the acclaimed TV series Yellowjackets (2021–present), blending psychological drama with genre elements.

Through her bold narratives and commitment to character-driven stories, Khouri has paved the way for women in mainstream cinema and television. Her work continues to inspire writers and filmmakers to tell fearless, original stories that resonate across generations.
