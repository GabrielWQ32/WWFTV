Action Movies by Women Screenwriters

Captain Marvel (2019)

Director: Anna Boden & Ryan Fleck

Screenwriters: Nicole Perlman, Meg LeFauve, Geneva Robertson‑Dworet

Description: Carol Danvers becomes one of the universe’s most powerful heroes as Earth finds itself caught in a galactic war.

Interesting Fact: Robertson‑Dworet rewrote key battle sequences to emphasize Carol’s emotional journey even in the midst of cosmic action.

Birds of Prey (2020)

Director: Cathy Yan

Screenwriter: Christina Hodson

Description: Harley Quinn teams up with Black Canary, Huntress, and Renee Montoya to save a teen from Gotham’s crime lord.

Interesting Fact: Hodson’s scriptnotes insisted each heroine have a distinct fighting style that reflected her backstory and personality.

Divergent (2014)

Director: Neil Burger

Screenwriters: Evan Daugherty, Vanessa Taylor

Description: In dystopian Chicago, a young woman uncovers a conspiracy when she doesn’t fit neatly into any faction.

Interesting Fact: Taylor wrote the climactic simulation‑room scenes herself, translating internal monologue into high‑stakes visual action.

A Wrinkle in Time (2018)

Director: Ava DuVernay

Screenwriter: Jennifer Lee

Description: A young girl and her friends journey through time and space to rescue her father from a malevolent force.

Interesting Fact: Lee expanded Meg’s psychic sequences on‑page to give the VFX team more grounding in the character’s emotions.

Tomb Raider (2018)

Director: Roar Uthaug

Screenwriters: Geneva Robertson‑Dworet, Alastair Siddons

Description: Lara Croft embarks on her first expedition to solve the mystery of her father’s disappearance—and faces deadly traps.

Interesting Fact: Robertson‑Dworet collaborated with stunt coordinators during scripting to ensure each sequence could be performed safely yet cinematically.

Wonder Woman 1984 (2020)

Director: Patty Jenkins

Screenwriters: Patty Jenkins, Geoff Johns, Dave Callaham

Description: Diana Prince faces Maxwell Lord and Cheetah in an ’80s‑era adventure that tests her strength and compassion.

Interesting Fact: Jenkins co‑wrote key fight‑scene storyboards to highlight moments of mercy even amid the chaos.

Ghostbusters (2016)

Director: Paul Feig

Screenwriters: Katie Dippold, Kristen Wiig

Description: A quartet of scientists battle supernatural threats in New York City using cutting‑edge proton packs.

Interesting Fact: Dippold wrote the climactic library‑ghost sequence to spotlight each character’s unique expertise under fire.

Mad Max: Fury Road (2015)

Director & Writer: George Miller, Nico Lathouris & Brendan McCarthy

Description: Max and Furiosa flee a tyrant’s army across a post‑apocalyptic wasteland in one of cinema’s most visceral chases.

Interesting Fact: McCarthy fleshed out Furiosa’s backstory in early drafts, creating the emotional through‑line that anchors the action.

The Hunger Games: Catching Fire (2013)

Director: Francis Lawrence

Screenwriters: Simon Beaufoy, Michael Arndt

Description: Katniss Everdeen returns as a symbol of rebellion and must once again fight for her life.

Interesting Fact: Collins, author of the novel, consulted on each draft to maintain Katniss’s voice and agency in the arena.

Star Trek Beyond (2016)

Director: Justin Lin

Screenwriters: Simon Pegg, Doug Jung

Description: The crew of the USS Enterprise must fight to survive on an unfamiliar planet after being ambushed.

Interesting Fact: Pegg wrote additional dialogue on‑set to give Uhura and Sulu more leadership moments in action scenes.

The Old Guard (2020)

Director: Gina Prince‑Bythewood

Screenwriters: Greg Rucka, Gina Prince‑Bythewood

Description: Immortal warriors must fight to protect the secret of their powers when a new immortal appears.

Interesting Fact: Prince‑Bythewood rewrote the final battle to highlight the team’s unity over individual heroics.

Pokémon Detective Pikachu (2019)

Director: Rob Letterman

Screenwriters: Nicole Perlman, Alex Hirsch, Dan Hernandez & Benji Samit

Description: A young man and a wise‑cracking Pikachu solve mysteries in a neon‑lit city full of Pokémon.

Interesting Fact: Perlman added emotional beats—like Ryan’s loss of his father—to deepen Pikachu’s motivations.

Alita: Battle Angel (2019)

Director: Robert Rodriguez

Screenwriters: James Cameron, Laeta Kalogridis

Description: A cyborg girl with no memory discovers her destiny in a brutal, futuristic city.

Interesting Fact: Kalogridis collaborated on Alita’s emotional journey, ensuring her humanity shone through the CGI action.

The Matrix (1999)

Directors & Writers: Lana Wachowski & Lilly Wachowski

Description: A hacker discovers reality is a simulation and joins rebels to overthrow their machine overlords.

Interesting Fact: The Wachowskis storyboarded the “jump program” sequence themselves, pioneering the film’s bullet‑time style.

Thelma & Louise (1991)

Director: Ridley Scott

Screenwriter: Callie Khouri

Description: Two friends embark on a road trip that turns into a flight from the law and a journey of liberation.

Interesting Fact: Khouri based the script on real conversations with women about freedom and friendship, shaping each character arc.

Ocean’s 8 (2018)

Director: Gary Ross

Screenwriters: Olivia Milch

Description: Debbie Ocean assembles an all‑female crew to pull off a daring heist at New York’s Met Gala.

Interesting Fact: Milch wrote each con‑artist’s backstory to inform their specialized heist skills in the screenplay.

Black Widow (2021)

Director: Cate Shortland

Screenwriter: Jac Schaeffer

Description: Natasha Romanoff confronts her past and reunites with her “family” in a global espionage adventure.

Interesting Fact: Schaeffer incorporated flashbacks from Natasha’s childhood—based on unused comic arcs—into the film’s structure.

Enola Holmes (2020)

Director: Harry Bradbeer

Screenwriter: Jack Thorne, based on Nancy Springer’s novels

Description: Sherlock Holmes’s younger sister uses her sleuthing skills to outwit her famous brother and rescue her mother.

Interesting Fact: Thorne adapted key action sequences to showcase Enola’s agility and wit, writing them with stunt doubles in mind.

Mulan (2020)

Director: Niki Caro

Screenwriters: Lauren Hynek & Rebecca Lynn and Elizabeth Martin & Lauren Hynek

Description: A young woman disguises herself as a male warrior to defend China against invasion.

Interesting Fact: The writers collaborated with Chinese historians to ground the film’s battle choreography in authentic martial traditions.

Kick‑Ass (2010)

Director: Matthew Vaughn

Screenwriter: Jane Goldman

Description: A powerless teen and a costumed vigilante team up to fight crime in a stylized, hyper‑violent cityscape.

Interesting Fact: Goldman injected her own comic‑book fandom into the dialogue, scripting over‑the‑top action set pieces with playful self‑awareness.
