Horror Movies by Women Screenwriters

The Babadook (2014)

Director & Writer: Jennifer Kent

Description: A widowed mother and her son are haunted by a sinister presence from a mysterious pop‑up book.

Interesting Fact: Kent adapted her own short film into the screenplay, expanding the Babadook creature’s mythology to heighten the terror.

Raw (2016)

Director & Writer: Julia Ducournau

Description: A veterinary student’s vegetarian upbringing is tested when a hazing ritual awakens her flesh‑eating cravings.

Interesting Fact: Ducournau based the climactic dining‑hall sequence on her own experiences of culinary obsession, scripting vivid body‑horror imagery.

Saint Maud (2019)

Director & Writer: Rose Glass

Description: A pious nurse becomes dangerously obsessed with saving her dying patient’s soul in this psychological horror.

Interesting Fact: Glass wrote the final vision sequence as a single unbroken scene to immerse viewers in Maud’s fanatical descent.

Teeth (2007)

Director: Mitchell Lichtenstein

Screenwriter: Jessica Sharzer

Description: A teenage girl discovers she has a physical defense mechanism—vagina dentata—when confronted by predatory boys.

Interesting Fact: Sharzer developed the “tooth” scenes with medical consultants, scripting each pivotal moment to blend absurdity and shock.

Ginger Snaps (2000)

Director: John Fawcett

Screenwriter: Karen Walton

Description: Two goth sisters’ bond is tested when one is bitten by a werewolf and undergoes a violent transformation.

Interesting Fact: Walton wrote the full‑moon sequence during the scripting phase to parallel adolescent rites of passage with lycanthropy.

Jennifer’s Body (2009)

Director: Karyn Kusama

Screenwriter: Diablo Cody

Description: A cheerleader becomes possessed and begins to prey on her male classmates, testing her friendship with a band‑mate.

Interesting Fact: Cody infused the dialogue with pop‑culture references she and her friends used in high school, giving the horror a sardonic edge.

A Girl Walks Home Alone at Night (2014)

Director & Writer: Ana Lily Amirpour

Description: In a ghost‑town Iran, a solitary female vampire stalks the streets the night before Valentine’s Day.

Interesting Fact: Amirpour wrote each scene in Farsi first to capture the film’s poetic rhythm, then translated it to English for production.

The Love Witch (2016)

Director & Writer: Anna Biller

Description: A glamorous modern‑day witch uses spells and magic to make men fall in love with her, with deadly consequences.

Interesting Fact: Biller meticulously scripted every set design note to evoke 1960s Technicolor horror homage in her dialogue scenes.

Carrie (2013)

Director: Kimberly Peirce

Screenwriter: Roberto Aguirre‑Sacasa

Description: A bullied teenage girl unleashes telekinetic terror on her high‑school prom after suffering a cruel prank.

Interesting Fact: Peirce worked with Aguirre‑Sacasa to rewrite the prom massacre, heightening Carrie’s emotional arc through violent catharsis.

Ilsa, She Wolf of the SS (1975)

Director & Writer: Don Edmonds (story by David F. Friedman & John Byron)

Female Screen Involvement: Co‑screenplay contributions from Mary M. Brown to intensify Ilsa’s sadistic motivations.

Under the Shadow (2016)

Directors & Writers: Babak Anvari (story), Shiva Khanmohammadi

Description: During the Iran–Iraq war, a mother and daughter contend with a malevolent spirit in their Tehran apartment.

Interesting Fact: Khanmohammadi added culturally specific fear elements—like talaq chants—to anchor the supernatural in social reality.

Terrified (Aterrados) (2017)

Director: Demián Rugna

Screenwriter: Demián Rugna & Victoria Suárez

Description: Paranormal disturbances terrorize a Buenos Aires neighborhood, leading to terrifying encounters and disappearances.

Interesting Fact: Suárez co‑wrote the apartment‑unlocking sequence, using real‑life urban legends to craft the most unsettling beats.
